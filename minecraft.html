The History of Minecraft

Introduction

Minecraft is a sandbox video game developed and published by Mojang Studios. Initially created by Markus "Notch" Persson, it was later purchased by Microsoft in 2014. Since its release, Minecraft has become one of the best-selling video games of all time, with over 200 million copies sold across all platforms as of 2021.

2009 - Inception

The development of Minecraft began in May 2009 when Markus Persson, a programmer from Sweden, started working on a project called "Cave Game". The project was renamed to "Minecraft: Order of the Stone" later and then simply Minecraft. The game was inspired by several different games, including Dwarf Fortress, Infiniminer, and RollerCoaster Tycoon.

2011 - Alpha and Beta Releases

Minecraft's alpha version was released on May 17, 2009, and was available to the public for purchase. The beta version of Minecraft launched in December 2010, with major improvements to the game’s mechanics, world generation, and more. The game's initial release was on November 18, 2011.

2014 - Microsoft Acquisition

In 2014, Markus Persson sold Mojang Studios to Microsoft for $2.5 billion. Following this, Microsoft continued to develop and expand Minecraft, including releasing updates to the game and introducing cross-platform play. They also introduced "Minecraft: Education Edition" to promote learning in classrooms.

Modern Day

Today, Minecraft is one of the most popular games in the world, with a massive fan base, numerous community-driven projects, and a huge impact on gaming culture. It has expanded to various platforms, including PC, consoles, and mobile devices. The game has seen multiple updates over the years, adding new content, features, and mechanics that keep it fresh and engaging for players.
